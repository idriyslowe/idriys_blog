Study App: Studere!

Studere is the name of my final project. Yes, I know that name is cheesy but I think I was picking fun of myself before anyone else got the chance. At the midway point in the bootcamp, we were supposed to have conceived of a project idea to present to strangers at the end of the course and all I could think about was how hard it was to organize my studies. How about I make an application that helps me organize myself? Easy, right?

Well, it wasn't easy to wrap my mind around designing and building a whole web application myself. At this point in my training, I just learned what something like that would take and I didn't have to apply much of that knowlege yet without being prompted step by step. I'll describe this beast of a project for you kind folks as best I can.

My first order of business was creating a spreadsheet to collect all of my thoughts. You don't know this about me (unless I'm a worse writer than I thought) but my brain is a circus. Focus to me loosely translates to "gain inspiration from this thing and then move on". Much of what I write here will be a re-interpretation of my notes which were broken down to four sheets in one Googlesheet document.

I won't bother sharing an image here (you know what lists of words look like) but for your knowlege, the sheets were called:

Concept:

Listed the features I needed for an MVP.

Tables:

Included simple wireframe, Model descriptions, Any nitty-gritty data-related notes.

Notes:

Listed tasks STEP BY STEP (I mean it) and hanging questions.

Done:

Just like it sounds. Listed all completed tasks in case I needed to go back and check a detail.
