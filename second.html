WELCOME TO ME!

The Idriys-life is full of wonders... and RidiCulouSssss...ness

Bootcamp

I'm midway through the second week of in-person classes.

My brain hurts so good.

Finally... I've seen Rails.

We began last Sunday (November 8, 2015) we sped through a rough intro to HTML and CSS in class. I got the gist of inline vs. block-level elements. I got the gist of nesting elements within eachother or marking elements with classes or ids for the sake of grouping and styling later on... but boy was I bored. Nothing felt like it was really clicking. Why?

I realized very quickly that most of my frustration and boredom with anything ever has everything to do with not having a reason. There was no pet project. No real ulitmate goal. I didn't even have real competition. I don't like to design. I like to win!

“What does it mean to be the best? It means you have to be better than the number two guy. But what gratification is there in that? He's a loser—that’s why he's number two.” -Jarod Kintz

No, but in all seriousness you can just google most of the HTML or CSS you need so long as you get the syntax and keep a consistent workflow. I'll touch more on design as I get a better hang of it.
